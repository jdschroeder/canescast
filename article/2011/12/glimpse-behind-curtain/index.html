A glimpse behind the curtain

December 30, 2011 - by Josh Schroeder

Have you ever wondered how we provide the coverage of Hurricane hockey home and away? Well, since today is a rare Saturday off for the Hurricanes, I’m available to give you a rundown of what we do and some of the tools that we use.

For home games, we have a great view of the ice from our seats in the new Steve ‘Foggy’ Fallwell Media Centre:

Currently we work a rotation where one of three of us will be doing the game updates on a given night. When the Hurricanes are on the road, we need to rely on the WHL’s video feed from whl.neulion.com:

You probably know that we post all of our updates to this web site on the Lethbridge Web Design Game Portal as well as our Twitter stream. The updates that go into the game portal also include some extra information like the period, time, and what type of event has occurred. We handle posting these updates with a custom application that we call CanesCaster.

It’s complete with plenty of shortcut keys, so we can compose our updates without our fingers leaving the keyboard, or our eyes leaving the ice (and now you know why we have so many typos). CanesCaster sends updates to the Game Portal, which then relays the content of those updates to Twitter.

Here’s what my computer typically looks like while I’m covering a road game:

You can see that I also have the WHL game summary page open for that game. It’s helpful mainly for referencing opposing team jersey numbers and making sure I spell names correctly. This also comes in handy for when I’m writing my game summary after the fact.

And photos too

The home games are where it gets more fun, because we also get to take photos. You may have seen us at the games, wearing CanesCast jackets and lugging around camera gear. If you see a guy between the benches taking pictures, there’s a good chance that it’s me. I like to spend time between the benches, because it’s just so close to the action (plus I get to hear the prime trash talk between the benches):

On special occasions you may even see me on the ice, trying my best not to fall down and provide any extra entertainment to you 3000 spectators. (Did you catch my near miss on teddy bear toss night? I know Brady Ramsay noticed and was ready to point and laugh.)

Between periods, I rush up to the press box, choose the shots that I like, either on my laptop or my iPad, and upload them to our server where a script handles watermarking and uploading to our Flickr stream, as well as sharing full-sized images for use by the Hurricanes and the WHL.

We keep pretty busy on game nights, so we’ve tried to build a workflow that lets us get as much done with as little friction as possible. We’ve come a long way from when we started nearly five seasons ago, only covering home games and updating directly to Twitter on our phones. Because of the improvements we’ve made, we’re able to bring our Caniac friends more coverage without burning ourselves out. I hope you enjoyed this glimpse into our world, and that you enjoy the updates that we bring you game in and game out.

Josh Schroeder

Josh’s hockey life has been intertwined with the Hurricanes since he took to the ice for training camp in 1999. Since then, he has spent countless hours in the Enmax Centre, occupying seats in section OO, the press box, and that tiny space between the players’ benches. In 2011 he designed the Hurricanes’ popular Hawker Hurricane third jersey emblem.

As a member of the CanesCast team, Josh builds and maintains CanesCast.ca, writes articles, and shoots game photos.
