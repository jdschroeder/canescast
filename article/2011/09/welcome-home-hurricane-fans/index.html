Welcome home, Hurricane fans

September 26, 2011 - by Josh Schroeder

Here we are, days away from the home opener. We're all excited to get a look at the new team on the ice, but we also have another summer's worth of renovations to the ENMAX Centre to take in. We got to take a tour of the building last week, and I'd like to give you a sneak peek at what you can expect to see when you arrive.

Video scoreboard

The $1.2 million investment is sure to be the most noticeable improvement to the building. Fans will now be able to enjoy replays and a wider range of in-game entertainment on the video screens, while the time and score will be more clearly readable as well. In-game promotions like the 50/50 and the Backstreet Bonus Die will also take advantage of the new video capabilities.

Improved concourse and concessions

The renovations have continued to open more space on the concourse, which should make traversing the crowds and getting your snacks between periods a little easier.

Box seats

Several more box seats have been added. While the ones we saw were not yet complete, these new boxes are clearly more in line with what you would expect in an upper class facility. Some of these boxes have been added north of the existing boxes, while the move of the press box opens up more prime space between the blue lines on the west side.

Standing room

For those who enjoy standing along the railing during the game, you will now have a countertop and coat hook. This is a welcome addition.

Party deck

The south end of the rink will feature a party deck accommodating 150-200 people for a lively game night atmosphere.

Lounge

Although this will not be complete until sometime in 2012, the north end of the rink will soon feature a lounge with table views of the ice.

Hurricane fans have plenty to look forward to this season with the exciting young team taking to the ice, and with these recent upgrades to the rink, there will just be that more more to enjoy about a night at the game.

Josh Schroeder

Josh’s hockey life has been intertwined with the Hurricanes since he took to the ice for training camp in 1999. Since then, he has spent countless hours in the Enmax Centre, occupying seats in section OO, the press box, and that tiny space between the players’ benches. In 2011 he designed the Hurricanes’ popular Hawker Hurricane third jersey emblem.

As a member of the CanesCast team, Josh builds and maintains CanesCast.ca, writes articles, and shoots game photos.
