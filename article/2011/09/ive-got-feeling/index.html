I've got a feeling....

September 22, 2011 - by Mark Huxley

All I hear at the moment is how sad people are that the weather is cooling off, the days are getting shorter and winter is around the corner.

You know what? I don’t care!

I never lament the loss of summer, but cherish the cooler nights as with it comes a season full of action, excitement and adventure. A season full of promise and expectation - it’s hockey season!

I love the buzz of the Enmax, as the boys take the ice for the pre-game skate, the nervous excitement as the lights dim and the first few beats of the Black Eyed Peas reverberate around the arena, the promise of what the night holds… this is what we live for Caniacs!

On this, our 25th year, there promises to be lots of surprises for the fans with the front office having worked hard lining up lots of events to celebrate our quarter century.

We have the awesome new video board at centre ice to live and relive all the highlights of the game, a new and improved Enmax centre with extensive renovations to add to your comfort and enjoyment.

You will not be disappointed when you see the improvements; we now have a world-class arena to watch our team in.

On the ice, the team looks to have lots of promise as some of our veterans move on and the raw talent of our younger guys take to the ice to show us that behind the scenes the hard work of good scouting, awesome draft picks and roster strengthening, has not been in vain.

Having attended the training camp and watched the exhibition and pre-season games, the future is bright for the ‘Canes with our younger players not looking out of place playing along side some of our more experienced players.

In fact, the wait to see some of them skate may not be too far away as GM and head coach, Rich Preston looks to bring in new faces, add depth and strengthen our roster as he builds a winning team.

Congratulations to Cam Braes who follows several WHL greats in becoming captain of your Lethbridge Hurricanes. In all honesty, Cam has been a leader on the ice for a couple of seasons now and fully deserves the honour of being given the captaincy for the 2011/12 season.

The future is bright in Lethbridge and I fully expect so see us driving towards the play-offs and going on the run our hard work deserves.

One thing for sure, when the puck drops in Medicine Hat to open the season on September 24th, the Canes will be firing on all cylinders as they get the jump-start on our 25th season in the WHL.

I can’t wait, can you?

Come on boys; make us proud – Go Canes Go!

Mark Huxley

Mark's arrival in Lethbridge coincided with the memorable 2008 playoff run. Needless to say, he was hooked on junior hockey and he's not looked back since.

CanesCast's game day play-by-play is primarily operated by Mark, and he also contributes as a writer and photographer.
