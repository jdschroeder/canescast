Freeze Frames: The Wrong Kind of Streak

October 20, 2011 - by Josh Schroeder

In our second instalment of Freeze Frames, we take a look at last Saturday's blow-out at the hands of the Swift Current Broncos, and a tough loss to the Kootenay Ice that stretched the Lethbridge Hurricanes' losing streak to nine games.

Steve "Foggy" Fallwell Night

Saturday's game got off to a nice start as the team honoured former play-by-play man Steve "Foggy" Fallwell, naming the new media centre for him. It was appropriate that the Broncos were in town, since "Foggy" was the voice of the Broncos during their tenure in Lethbride as well.

Brody Sutter presented Fallwell with a gift during the pre-game ceremony.

WHL commissioner Ron Robison was in attendance and gave a few words on behalf of the league.

A new sign was unveiled on the south end of the press box to honour Fallwell's contribution to the team.

Several former members of the Hurricanes and Broncos sent video messages, including Duane Sutter on behalf of all of the Sutter brothers who played in Lethbridge.

The game got off to a good start, the Hurricanes taking a 2-0 lead in the first period, but the defensive breakdowns started after Damien Ketlo left the game due to a lower body injury.

Jaimen Yakubowski challenges a Bronco player for the puck.

Macoy Erkamps keeps inside position on a forward in the Hurricanes' zone.

Brody Sutter, still sporting a full cage, carries the puck through the neutral zone.

We won't be seeing much of this guy anymore. This would be Reid Jackson's last game as a Hurricane. He was traded to Prince George the next day.

With the game already out of reach, Sam Mckechnie gave the fans something to cheer about, cutting in from the right side and flipping one past Smith for his first WHL goal.

This one seemed like it summed up the emotional feel of the game.

First look at the new guys

In the wake of Saturday's loss, Rich Preston was busy on Sunday, making three trades, sending out Derek Ryckman (Tri-City), Reid Jackson (Prince George), and Brandon Anderson (Brandon) and brining in Liam Liston, Spencer Galbraith, and Nick Buonassisi. Liston and Galbraith made their debuts Tuesday against the Ice.

Galbraith dumping the puck in from center ice.

Perhaps my favourite shot of the night has Jamal Watson dumping a Kootenay player to the ice.

Although, I also like this celebration after Landon Oslanski cut the deficit to 3-2 in the third period.

It was a good effort, and the new guys looked good. We came close to seeing Galbraith fight with Brock Montgomery late in the game after he dealt Graham Hood a shot behind the Kootenay net. On Friday night we'll get our first look at Nick Buonassisi as well, as the Hurricanes try to halt their October losing streak against the Kamloops Blazers (7-3).
