Already out of the race?

October 21, 2011 - by Josh Schroeder

Twelve games and ten losses into the season, it's a fair question to ask: are the Lethbridge Hurricanes already out of this year's WHL playoff race?

Early in this losing skid, I was defending the team's chances, insisting that they would turn a corner and be a competitive team down the stretch. That could very well still happen, but it will take much more than competitive to have a hope of overcoming the hole that they have dug for themselves over the past four weeks.

On his blog at CBC.ca, Elliotte Friedman took a look at recent NHL history and how teams have fared when falling behind in the first month of the season. Spoiler: it's not good news if you're more than three points back. Gregg Drinnan has already discussed Friedman's article as it relates to the Hurricanes, but I'd like to dig a little further into just what it would take for the Hurricanes to make the post season.

Looking at the situation purely from a numbers perspective, the current pace of the top eight teams in the WHL's Eastern Conference is above a .630 win percentage, which would equate to just about 92 points to qualify for the playoffs. You'll remember that in our previous article on setting expectations we considered 75 points a reasonable playoff target, so the current pace is much more competitive than it has been over the past several years.

If that exceptional pace continues, the Hurricanes would need 87 more points in the final 60 games to crack the top eight. At a .725 win percentage, the Hurricanes would need to win almost three of every four games. That's as many as 45 wins–the same amount they won in the entire 2007/08 season en route to the Eastern Conference championship.

To even reach 75 points, which is looking like a very low projection that would not qualify them for the playoffs, the Hurricanes would need a win percentage of .583 for the remainder of the season. Achieving that would require as much as 35 more wins–12 more than they won all of last season.

Should they be able to stay healthy and get their act together in their own zone, I think the 75 point target could still be an outside possibility. Anything near 92 points, though, would be a miraculous turn of events.

I was hoping we wouldn't be drawing this sort of conclusion this season, especially only a month into the campaign, but nothing short of a remarkable turnaround from the Hurricanes will bring WHL playoff hockey back to Lethbridge for 2012.
