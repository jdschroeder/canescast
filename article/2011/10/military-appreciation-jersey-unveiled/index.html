Military appreciation jersey unveiled

October 25, 2011 - by Josh Schroeder

The Lethbridge Hurricanes unveiled a military-inspired jersey today to pay tribute to Canadian servicemen and women. The Hurricanes and Glenn Miller, Service Officer with General Stewart Branch #4, were part of the reveal at the Cenotaph outside of Yates Theatre. The lucky Hurricane to model the new uniform was Graham Hood.


He looks like he's done this before, doesn't he?

The jersey sports a pattern consistent with the Hurricanes' current red jerseys, but features a camouflage pattern, replaces the logo's stars with maple leafs, and adds a maple leaf to the right shoulder and a yellow Support the Troops ribbon on the left.

The jerseys will be worn on Military Appreciation Night, Wednesday, November 9 against the Seattle Thunderbirds. If you'd like to own one of these limited edition jerseys, you will be able to bid on one at the Hurricanes' online auction house, HurricanesAuction.com between November 3 and 5:00 PM on November 14. Proceeds from the auction will be split between the Poppy Fund and and a designated military initiative to help with the establishment of the local military museum.

Personally, this isn't my favourite special edition jersey. It's hard to compete with the standard set by the Maple Leafs jerseys from January, and the camouflage doesn't really do it for me. That said, I think swapping out the stars for maple leafs was a nice touch, and I appreciate that the overall look remains consistent with the Hurricanes' regular uniforms.
