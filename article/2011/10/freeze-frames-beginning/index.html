Freeze Frames: The Beginning

October 04, 2011 - by Josh Schroeder

This post is the first in a series that I will be running this season called Freeze Frames. These articles will provide some insight into the photos I take at Hurricane games throughout the year, highlighting some of my favourites and giving a little bit of narrative along the way.

I would like to acknowledge Seattle Thunderbirds team photographer Kyle Scholzen for unknowingly providing the inspiration to take on this series. I have been following his blog, entitled Bird Watching, for the last year or so and have very much enjoyed it. I hope that you enjoy my photos as much as I enjoy Kyle's.

Let's kick things off with the home opener. Last season's player introductions produced one of my favourite photos of the season (As a side note, I've been using that photo all year as my iPhone wallpaper. Since Machacek is now a Thunderbird, though, I've finally swapped it out with a new one of Sutter). Unfortunately, this year's player introductions featured no pyrotechnics, fog, or any other particular visual flare; Just the spotlight. I began the evening back by the player entrance, hoping to get a nice dramatic silhouette of a player taking to the ice. The result was far less exciting than I was hoping for.

After mostly just getting in the video crew's way, I decided to head down to the far end of the rink and get ready for the first period. From there I managed to catch the tail end of player introductions.

From there I kept things pretty simple for the first period, shooting from the Zamboni gate. Here we've got Albin Blomqvist delivering a hit on a Hitmen player.

My favourite shot of the night features Juraj Bezuch celebrating a goal in the first period.

Brody Sutter scored a beautiful breakaway goal on a lead pass from Derek Ryckman. Sutter couldn't help but take a peek at his goal on the new video screen.

Toward the end of the period I headed up to the press box to process a few photos and decided to check out the feasibility of shooting from our seats up there. I think it could work.

The second period was similar positioning, this time with the Hurricanes moving towards me. That set up a few good shots of the d-men, like this one of Reid Jackson unloading a slap shot.

For the third period I headed down to my favourite [off-ice] spot in the rink: the suicide box.

Among the things I love about shooting from here are being able to hear and feel the game so much more than from anywhere else in the rink. It's the best place to feel a part of the game. Sometimes you almost do become part of the game. As you can imagine, I was back-peddling as Cam Braes almost came to join me in the box. I'm happy to report that so far I've always escaped the box unscathed.

Situated right on the red line, there is plenty of action moving towards you.

Meanwhile you are also afforded a pretty good look at the goalmouths.

As a nice finish to the home opener, rookie Jay Merkley was named first star for his two goal performance.

And how about a bonus shot from the warm-up of Jaimen Yakubowski showing off by juggling a puck on his stick.

I went a little easier for Saturday's game against the Pats, but started off with this one from the press box early on.

The shooting angle from the press box makes it more difficult to see faces, but it does open up some nice clean white backgrounds.

We saw a little bit of rough stuff, but no real scraps breaking out in this game.

It was good to be back in the rink, taking in the hockey and shooting photos again. I look forward to sharing more with you as the season rolls on.
