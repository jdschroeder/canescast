Breaking down a busy trade day

October 16, 2011 - by Josh Schroeder

The Lethbridge Hurricanes' losing streak has hit eight games, and has now claimed its first casualties. Rich Preston has been working the phones lately, it would seem, and the result is going to be a very different look to the Hurricanes' blue line. Let's take a look at each of the trades in the order in which the news broke:

Lethbridge sends Derek Ryckman (D) to Tri-City for a 4th round pick in 2012

I must admit that I'm a little confused by this move. On a team that is struggling so badly in their own zone, sending off a veteran stay-at-home defenceman and getting no current assets back in return seems rather unproductive. I'm struggling to see how this trade improves the Hurricanes, except perhaps through some intangible shake-up factor.

Lethbridge sends Reid Jackson (D) to Prince George for Nick Buonassisi (C)

This trade I like better because the Hurricanes are able to address an area of immediate need by adding Buonassisi. A centerman with three full WHL seasons under his belt and a solid 53-point season last year, it figures that Buonassisi should be able to improve the Hurricanes offensive production, and perhaps some new blood on the powerplay can help to inject some life into that area of the special teams as well.

Lethbridge sends Brandon Anderson (G) and 3rd and 6th round picks in 2012 to Brandon for Liam Liston (G) and Spencer Galbraith (D)

Here we have the trade that everybody knew was coming. Moving one of the veteran goaltenders was obviously a priority for Rich Preston, despite how the situation may have been muddied by Damien Ketlo's injury on Saturday evening. I am somewhat confused that addressing a crowded crease involves acquiring an 18 year old goaltender, and I'll be interested to see where each of the goaltenders fits in this scenario. Spencer Galbraith ought to add a little bit of toughness that has been absent from the Hurricanes' blue line so far this season; YouTube testifies that he's not afraid to get involved with his fists.

The bottom line

The Hurricanes gave up a fair bit today, including two players who attended NHL camps at the start of this season. I don't feel like the roster has been considerably improved by these moves, but the shake-up may be what's needed to force the team out of this terrible funk in which they currently find themselves. Ultimately, I still think there is a void a defence that needs to be addressed, so I wouldn't be surprised to see another move to that effect before too long.

Hurricane fans should get their first look at Liston and Galbraith on Monday night against the Ice, while Buonassisi's debut won't be until Friday against Kamloops.
