Give Thanks

October 17, 2013 - by Josh Schroeder

In the spirit of Thanksgiving this week, I thought it appropriate to share some things that Hurricane fans have to be thankful for in the midst of the team's slow start and current seven game losing streak.

1. It’s always darkest before the dawn

Four years without playoff hockey and a 1-9-0-1 start to this season. At some point the Hurricanes will start winning games with regularity, so we have that to look forward to.

2. A brand of our own

Finally, the Capitals wordmark is gone from the Hurricanes’ sweaters which are also absent any Warner Brothers trademarked characters. Early reaction to the new logo was mixed, but even with my lukewarm feelings toward it, I am pleased that the team has its own visual identity that is suitable to represent the club for years to come.

3. The goalie of the future

For a team that has struggled for the past five years to develop a goaltending prospect in-house (Tai and/or Hogue may still prove me wrong), it is exciting to potentially see a starter on the horizon who won’t be 20 years old when he joins the Hurricanes. First round pick Stuart Skinner had a strong training camp and pre-season appearance and is off to a 3-0 start in the AMHL this season with a .920 save percentage. All the signs currently point to Skinner being the Hurricanes’ goalie of the future

4. Ryan Pilon’s birthday

In particular, his late birthday that makes him ineligible for the 2014 NHL entry draft.

This is how the NHL collective bargaining agreement defines player age:

As used in this Article, "age 18" means a Player reaching his eighteenth birthday between January 1 next preceding the Entry Draft and September 15 next following the Entry Draft, both dates included

Since Pilon does not turn 18 until next October, he will be considered 17 for the purposes of the 2014 entry draft. That means Hurricane fans will potentially get to see him play one more season in the WHL than if he had been born a month earlier. Pilon should hear his name called early in the 2015 entry draft.

5. We don't live in Medicine Hat

The Tigers may be a better team right now, but they're in Medicine Hat, and that’s just not worth it.
