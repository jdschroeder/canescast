Training Camp Notebook

September 01, 2013 - by Josh Schroeder

Training camp has now wound up for the weekend, and we’ve now had an opportunity to get a look at this year’s crop of Hurricane prospects and hopefuls. I spent most of Saturday down at the rink taking in the action and compiling some thoughts.

Returning players

I didn’t see a whole lot of surprises among the returning players. Brady Ramsay and Jaimen Yakubowski both showed a nose for the net, while Jamal Watson and Sam Mckechnie skated fast in straight lines. One returning player who I thought stood out, though, was Remi Laurencelle. He was dominant in one-on-one drills, and was flying during the scrimmage. If yesterday’s performance is any indication, I think we will see good things from Laurencelle this year.

Rookies

Giorgio Estephan wasn’t outstanding in the way that I expected him to be. I thought I would see a performance more like what we saw from Reid Duke as a rookie in last year’s camp, but Estephan didn’t find himself in the middle of the action the way Duke did. Instead, Estephan showed a good knack for finding open space and distributing the puck quickly to create opportunities.

Chase Phelps is a big and fast forward with good vision. He looked good on a line with Duke, and the two skilled forwards played off of each other well to make things happen inside the blue line. He is pretty much what I expected of a guy who comes out of Shattuck St. Mary’s and played on the US National U-18 team this summer.

On defence, Kade Jensen stood out for me during the drills, showing excellent gap control and a surefire ability to shut down attacking forwards one-on-one. In the scrimmage he seemed to let his aggressive side get the better of him and took some needless penalties, but he also showed an ability to use his body to stand the opposition up at the blue line. He could be one of those on-the-edge players who is a huge asset when he can stay out of the penalty box.

Bryton Sayers, who put up outstanding numbers in Midget last year, looks like a WHLer already. He reads the play well, knowing when to move in off the point, and he plays a smooth, composed game. From what I saw of him, I have to think he will be a regular with the Hurricanes this season.

We now await news of roster trim downs ahead of Thursday’s exhibition game in Taber against the Calgary Hitmen. Don’t forget that tickets for that game are $10 and are available at Joe’s Skate Sharpening, Health Connection, or at the door of the Taber arena.
