The Numbers Game

September 22, 2013 - by Josh Schroeder

To go along with a handful of new faces on the Hurricanes’ roster this year, a few players have swapped jersey numbers. We’ll help you out here so you don’t have to keep checking the program during the game for the first month of the season.

The switchers

Rookies get a pretty limited selection when it comes to jersey numbers, so it’s pretty common that a guy will snag a preferred number when a player vacates it. These are the guys who you’ve become accustomed to in a certain number who now have a different identifier.

Ryan Pilon switches from #14, taking the more traditional defenceman number, #4, which was previously worn by Albin Blomqvist.

Tyler Wong switches from #8 and takes over Dan Johnston’s #5.

Axel Blomqvist changes from #26 and into Jay Merkley’s old #21.

The newcomers

And the rookies and trade acquisitions:

1 - Corbin Boes
8 - Bryton Sayers
23 - Kade Jensen
24 - Spencer Meyer
25 - Carter Amson
26 - Reid Nemeth
29 - Giorgio Estephan
33 - Jonny Hogue

The new look

If you’re anything like me, you immediately took notice of the new number style on the Hurricanes’ jerseys this year, too. Previously, the numbers were a pretty standard varsity-style block, as seen here on Graham Hood:

The new numbers are more styled, matching the Hurricanes’ wordmark:

It’s a bit of a subtle change, but one that gives the Hurricanes a more unique and professional look. The one gripe I have with it is that the names are still in a varsity-style block. I think it would look more cohesive if they used a slanted letterform as well. Also, I found it a little hard to differentiate between 8’s and 9’s on the video feed last night. I was relying on left hand vs. right hand to quickly tell Derko from Estephan at times. Other than that, though, it’s another incremental improvement in the Hurricanes’ aesthetic.
