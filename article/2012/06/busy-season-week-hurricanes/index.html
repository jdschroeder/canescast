Busy off-season week for the Hurricanes

June 28, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

It may be the off-season, but that doesn't mean Lethbridge Hurricanes head coach and GM Rich Preston is taking a holiday. After a flurry of activity this week, the Hurricanes' roster is taking on a different look.

Things got started on Tuesday when the Hurricanes released Slovakian winger Juraj Bezuch, opening up a roster spot for a pick in the next day's CHL import draft. With the 17th overall pick in that draft on Wednesday, the 'Canes added 6'5" right winger Axel Blomqvist. Sometimes there is some concern regarding whether an import draft will want to come to North America to play, but that shouldn't be a problem here, since Axel sounds excited to join his brother Albin here in Lethbridge.

Also of note from the import draft was Bezuch's selection by the OHL's Kitchener Rangers. He'll serve as an insurance policy in case Radek Faksa, a first round pick of the Dallas Stars, sticks in the NHL this season. The 'Canes sent their second round import pick to the Medicine Hat Tigers for an 9th round bantam draft pick.

Preston followed up the draft by making a splash in the trade market, dealing goaltender Liam Liston to the Vancouver Giants in exchange for 3rd and 6th round bantam draft picks. This move gives Liston an opportunity to take on a veteran starting role on a team with two young goalkeepers.

The Hurricanes then announced today that local product Max Moline will not return to the team next season and has opted to enroll at the University of Lethbridge instead. Moline tallied 2 goals and 2 assists in 21 games last season while being kept out of action with an injury for most of the season.

Finally, the Philadelphia Flyers announced their prospect camp roster today, and former Hurricane Austin Fyten will be taking part. You may recall that Fyten was to take part in the Flyers' rookie camp last season, but was denied the opportunity after injuring his knee in a pre-season game against Medicine Hat.

CanesCast would like to wish all of these former Hurricanes the best as they move on to the next phase of their hockey careers. Meanwhile, we welcome Axel Blomqvist to the Hurricane family.
