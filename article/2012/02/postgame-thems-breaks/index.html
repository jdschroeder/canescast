Postgame: Them's the Breaks

February 11, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

Back at the Enmax after falling short in an incomplete effort against the Moose Jaw Warriors on Wednesday night, the Hurricanes sought redemption against the Saskatoon Blades, a team fighting for position in the upper half of the WHL’s eastern conference standings.

Damien Ketlo gets his fourth consecutive start in goal, while the two imports, Juraj Bezuch and Albin Blomqvist get the night off as scratches.

What happened

The Hurricanes got off to a quick start, capitalizing on a Matej Stransky tripping penalty. Brody Sutter tipped a centering pass from Brady Ramsay past Andrey Makarov to give the home side a 1-0 lead just two minutes into the game. Halfway through the period Tyler Kizuik followed up his own rebound and buried his third goal of the year to give the Hurricanes a 2-0 lead. The lead was cut in half less than a minute later when a slow trickler ended up just out of reach of Adam Henry and Damien Ketlo, just inside the stick side post.

The lead disappeared four minutes into the second period when Jesse Paradis snuck another trickling puck past Ketlo for a powerplay goal to even the score at two apiece. Michael Burns put the Blades ahead with a powerplay goal with five minutes left in the period, and then Lukas Sutter advanced the lead just prior to the buzzer quickly releasing a backhander from the slot to make the score 4-2.

Matej Stransky extended the Blades’ lead nearly seven minutes into the third period, tallying Saskatoon’s fourth powerplay goal of the evening. Craig Leverton gave the Hurricanes some life, finishing off a a chance started by Jamal Watson. Stransky put it out of reach minutes later with his second of the night, sealing a 6-3 win for the visitors.

The Hurricanes ended up outshooting the Blades 35-33, earning numerous chances, but unable to capitalize on the chances they needed to come away with the win. Russ Maxwell, Graham Hood, and Sam McKechnie all had good chances from in close, and all were snakebitten on this night.

One good reason

...why the Hurricanes lost. Plain and simple, it was on the special teams tonight, specifically the penalty killing. Allowing the Blades to go 4/6 with the man advantage was the difference in this game.

SportPhotographers.ca Player of the Game

Jamal Watson takes the honour tonight, using his skill and speed to set up chances, including the one that resulted in Leverton’s third period goal.

Wrap-up

The home stand opens on a pair of losses against the top two teams in the eastern division. Saturday night the Hurricanes will entertain the last place team in the division, the Prince Albert Raiders. Game time is 7:00 at the Enmax Centre.
