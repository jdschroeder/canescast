In the Pipeline

February 29, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

We recently took a look at how the rookies have fared with the Hurricanes this season. Now, in light of the season ending for several midget teams this week, I’d like to check in on how some of the future prospects have been looking.

There isn’t much left to speak of from the 2009 draft class. McKechnie, Henry, Yakubowski, and Maxwell are all now mainstays on the Hurricane line-up, and two previously promising prospects, Bennefield and Guiel, are no longer property of the team and not looking likely to be impact WHLers. Harrison Harper is the wildcard in the group as a 12th round pick who remains in the picture. He’s spent the season with the Heritage junior B league’s Airdrie Thunder, while putting in a handful of games with the Hurricanes. Likely he’ll see some more action as he is with the Hurricanes on their current road trip. With 10 points in 28 games, he’s not exactly a superstar in the making, but could be a worthwhile option for depth on the blue line.

Let’s move on to players selected in the 2010 draft: the guys who were eligible for their first full season in the WHL this year. For some context, these are the current Hurricanes selected in that draft.

Topping and Laurencelle have been putting up strong numbers at the midget AAA level, and both earned spots in their respective leagues’ all-star games. Topping was in fact a first team all-star and an alternate captain for the the AMHL all-star game. We’ve seen him play a couple of games with the Hurricanes this season and it appears he’s well on course to be a WHLer next sesaon. While we haven’t seen Laurencelle playing against WHL competition this season, he’s put up good offensive numbers, and should be expected to challenge for a roster spot next year as well.

It will be interesting to see what sort of competition Ferguson can provide in the crease when training camp rolls around. He has put up good numbers this year, but not playing at the top tier of competition in the province.

Obviously, we’ve paid a great deal of attention to Pilon and Duke this season, and they’ve both been productive players who have been given a brief taste of WHL action. Duke, in particular, has fit in quite nicely in his appearances with the Hurricanes. I would be surprised if either of these two don’t crack the line-up as 16 year olds next year. Olynek, Sayers, and Wong have all put up good numbers as well, and Wong was one of only two 15 year olds named to the AMHL all-star game. It sounds like we’ll get another look at Wong on the Hurricanes’ current road trip, but based on his brief call-up earlier this season, he also stands a chance of cracking the roster as a 16 year old. Nemeth has put up outstanding numbers at the minor midget AAA level; he’ll likely be expected to stick at the AMHL level next season to continue his development. Chartier has had a tough season, missing time with a broken collarbone.

Hogue’s numbers were less than stellar, but unlike the year older Ferguson, he was playing top-tier AAA and backstopping the last place team in the south division. From what I saw in watching the Pronghorns this year, Hogue’s numbers may not be indicative of his actual ability, but more a by-product of some tough circumstances. Hogue did accompany the Hurricanes on a brief road trip a few weeks ago, but don’t read too deeply into that. I think the emergency call-up was more a matter of convenience, and Hogue will be best served by spending another year in midget next season.

Some of these players have been active beyond league play this season as well. Eight Hurricane prospects suited up in the Western Canadian U16 Challenge (Hogue, Duke, Nemeth, and Wong for tournament champion Alberta, and Pilon, Sayers, Amson, and Olynek with Saskatchewan). Duke and Pilon were also part of the bronze medal winning Canadian team at the Youth Olympics in Innsbruck, Austria, each tallying three goals and two assists in six games.

Early indications are that the Hurricanes’ 2011 draft class is a pretty strong group to follow up the similarly styled 2010 selections. Training camp is months away yet, and performance there can be influenced greatly by how a player spends his summer. Being as the team is already relatively young with few positions becoming available as a result of departures, it should make for a competitive 2012 camp.
