On the Oil Kings and Wheat Kings

Josh: It looks like the Wheat Kings’ goaltending has come around as Boes had a solid first round, and Kevin Sundher looks to be rounding back into form after his relocation and subsequent injury. I still like the Oil Kings’ depth, though, so I’m taking them to win a tight series.

Fred: This series I don't think will be a traditional 1 vs 6 matchup. For me the Wheat Kings have the best player in the series (Mark Stone) and a very hot goaltender in Corbin Boes. The Oil Kings were the best team in the regular season and deservedly earned that honor. However, lack of big game, and playoff experience will be a big factor working against the Kings in this one. I'll take the upset just like I did successfully in Round one. Wheat Kings in 6.

Darrell: I hate all things Edmonton. (Editor’s note: This we can agree on.)

On the Warriors and Tigers

Josh: I expect a very tight series in this one. The Tigers bring the star power, and the Warriors bring talent of their own with solid depth and leadership. The Warriors have been on fire at home this season and their home ice advantage could be the difference in the series.

Fred: Dear Mr. WHL Commissioner Ron Robison: Is there any chance of making this series a Best of 11???? Figured, no would be the answer but thought it was worth a shot. Every game in this matchup has the makings of being a classic. High end goaltending, underrated blue lines and for me the best 2 forward groups of the 4 second round series. This one could go either way, but home ice advantage matters. Moose Jaw finds a way to win it in 7.

Darrell: Love the way the tigers are playing right now, they're healthy and they've got a lot of fire power; thats not to take anything away from MJ. Pat will take MJ cause he's got a man-crush on Cam Braes.

On the Americans and Chiefs

Josh: No way the Chiefs can withstand the Americans’ onslaught with Kichton injured for the rest of the playoffs.

Fred: Taking favorites all the time doesn't put much of a fire in your belly. I'll leave the safe picks to some others offering predictions in this site. The Chiefs have a winning culture. They stormed back with 4 straight W's against Vancouver. Why stop now. Chiefs in 6.

Darrell: TC - Too good.

On the Winterhawks and Blazers

Josh: The Blazers have a debilitating injury of their own with Schaber done for the year. That’s a big loss, and the Winterhawks will be hard to keep pace with. Portland will win this series.

Fred: Portland is too well coached and too explosive. The Blazers will make the Hawks earn it, but I believe they find a way. Portland in 5.
