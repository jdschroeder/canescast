On the Oil Kings and Warriors

Josh: The Warriors are getting a boost with Morgan Reilly back for this series. I may be going with my heart in wanting to see Braes go out on top, but I'm taking Moose Jaw in a series that goes the distance.

Mark: Again another one that belies the easy way both teams have got here, with moose jaw losing a solitary game in the first 2 rounds and Edmonton unbeaten in 8.....Edmonton again in 7, but wouldn't be too bothered if our good friend Cam Braes goes on to win this with the warriors.

Esther: For as offensively good Edmonton is … as well as their D and solid goaltending, I’m sticking with my boy – Cam Braes and the Moose Jaw Warriors in 7 games.

Fred: After waiting nearly a month, we finally get the showdown matchup that we have been waiting for. Edmonton, is playing at an exceptionally high level right now, winners of 19 straight. Not only will the Warriors need to disrupt that incredible run, they will have to find a way to beat Derek Laxdal's team 4 times in the next 7 outings. Not an easy task, but it is manageable is you're Moose Jaw. The Warriors match up well in goal. I think Luke Siemens can hang in there with Laurent Brossoit who currently leads all WHL playoff netminders with the best Goals Against Average and Save Percentage. Moose Jaw's defence is steady and tough. Dylan Mcilrath, Kendall Mcfaull, and Joel Edmundson are a great 1-2-3 punch on the back end. Edmonton has Mark Pysyk and a strong supporting cast. Up front, the Oil Kings can score. I like the complete players Moose Jaw added at the trade deadline in James Henry and former Hurricane Captain Cam Braes. Lethbridge's best player during the first half of the season has been brilliant in the playoffs with points in all 9 games Moose Jaw has played. This will be a dandy of a series! I go with my heart in this one.

On the Americans and Winterhawks

Josh: Portland should be hungry after nearly squandering a 3-0 series lead against a short-manned Kamloops squad. I've been calling for Portland vs. Moose Jaw all along, so why stop now? Winterhawks in 6.

Mark: Could go either way, but I think tri city will win it in 7

Esther: Out west, come on Tri City!! Americans in 6.

Fred: These 2 teams are the most deserving clubs out west to be in this Championship series. You have the leagues top 3 playoff scorers squaring off in Portland's Ty Rattie and Sven Bartschi along with Tri Cities Brendan Shinnimin. I think Mike Johnston and Travis Green for the Winterhawks have the best coaching tandem in the league. For me, that will be the difference in this matchup.

Josh’s hockey life has been intertwined with the Hurricanes since he took to the ice for training camp in 1999.
