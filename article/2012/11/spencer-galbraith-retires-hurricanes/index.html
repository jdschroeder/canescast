Spencer Galbraith retires from the Hurricanes

November 09, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

As the Lethbridge Hurricanes have just announced, 19 year old defenceman Spencer Galbraith has made the decision to retire from the WHL.

Acquired from the Brandon Wheat Kings last year along with Liam Liston, Galbraith was currently experiencing his best season in terms of point production, having tallied 2 goals and 5 assists in 20 games. His seven points matched his full season totals from each of the last two seasons.

The departure of a veteran defenceman leaves a void at defence that will likely be filled by Joel Topping who has been a healthy scratch in a few recent games. Looking forward, this leaves the Hurricanes with only three players who will be entering their over age season next year (Albin Blomqvist, Brady Ramsay, and Michael Sofillas), so it is possible that we will see Rich Preston look to add another veteran before too long.

From all of us at CanesCast, we wish Spencer all the best with whatever he pursues after hockey, and thank him for his contributions to the team in the last year. We're going to miss those hip checks.
