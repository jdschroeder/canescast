Hood sidelined with a fractured hand

November 09, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

This picture was taken moments after the final buzzer in Lethbridge's 4-2 win over the Victoria Royals on Wednesday. Captain Graham Hood saluted the crowd with his teammates as is the tradition after a victory on home ice. The expression on his face told the story, though: he was hurting.

In the dying minutes of a one goal game, the Royals had pulled their goaltender for an extra attacker and were applying pressure in the Hurricanes' zone. Several times Victoria set up shots from the point, but the Hurricane forwards dropped into the shooting lanes to block the shots. Hood was the player to get in front of a couple of those shots, preserving the lead.

It's not uncommon for a player to suffer a stinger blocking these shots, leaving him hobbling to the bench to walk it off before returning for his next shift. Sometimes, though, it's worse, as was the case with Hood who will now miss a couple of weeks with a fractured hand, as reported by Pat Siedlecki.

It could be worse, though. Just ask Alexander Khokhlachev.

Or Patrick Thoresen whose infamous shot block is probably at least partially responsible for this common shot blocking pose demonstrated by Reid Duke:

Losing Hood is tough news, especially on the same day as Spencer Galbraith's departure, but fortunately it sounds like a minor fracture, so it shouldn't keep him out of action for too long.
