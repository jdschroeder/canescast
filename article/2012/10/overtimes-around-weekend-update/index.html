Overtime's Around - Weekend Update

October 23, 2012 - by Morgan Martin

Max FirePower. In deference to my colleague (good evening Mr Huxley) I have capitalized the P in firepower. An obvious Simpson's reference I am willing to piggy back on. The other more relevant reason for the title is the play of Russell Maxwell in tonights game. A dynamo the whole game; seemingly fit to play on any line combination, Russell was a highlight reel to himself. With 6 goals in the last 5 games Maxwell has been a force for the Canes in recent nights earning his chances on the top lines.

Ramsay's Return

Brady Ramsay has been another positive over the last few games. Brady's return adds another dimension of offensive skill, drive and determination to the Canes front. With a goal and 4 assists to close out his return to this point in the month, it's not hard to see that he has been a missing element from the team.

Rimmer Rest?

Ty Rimmer has been a work horse in his 12 games to date. Stellar on the weekend; he has been a solid back drop for a team that is now at the point they can leverage his play to put themselves into winning position. No doubt Ty would say he has lots more in the tank, but with hopes of going to the playoffs I would expect to see Ty take a game or two on the bench for some well deserved rest.

Spencer on the Wing

Spencer Galbraith put some time on the wing on Saturday and was definitely not out of place. With a goal on the night and the aggressive, in your face style of play he brings to the game Spencer brought some real dynamism to the game. A little bonus for those of us lucky enough to have been at the game.

Double OT

With 3 points out of 4 the Canes put on their best weekend showing so far this season. Clawing their way back from a 4 - 1 deficit on Friday against the CHL number one showed some of the skill and determination we devoted fans have always known was there. It didn't end their way on that night, but more was yet to come.

Saturday the Canes kept it close the whole way. Watson brought the goods with a semi breakaway effort he slid under a fooled Justin Paulic. Watson's effort tied the game with 8 to go in the third. Jaimen Yakubowski followed that up with a beauty to put the check in the W column for the Canes. Another gritty effort to round out an awesome weekend of hockey that keeps this reporter excited to keep coming back.
