Who will win the Ed Chynoweth Cup?

May 03, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

We're back for the last set of WHL playoff predictions from our panel. The series kicks off tonight in Edmonton with the Eastern Conference champion Edmonton Oil Kings hosting the Western Conference Portland Winterhawks. With the exception of Portland's seven game series against Kamloops, these teams have both cruised along pretty handily in the playoffs so far. The modern day Oil Kings are making their first appearance in the finals, while the Winterhawks are on a return trip after having lost the Ed Chynoweth Cup final to the Kootenay Ice last season.

Josh Schroeder - CanesCast (11-3)

Oil Kings in 6

These guys are absolutely on a roll, and while I think Portland will give them a bit of a challenge, I think they're simply too good right now to be beaten. I look forward to seeing how Portland's high end scoring trio of Rattie, Ross, and Baertschi fare against the stingy defence and goaltending of the Oil Kings. Laurent Brossoit has a 1.62 GAA, 0.945 save percentage, and two shutouts in these playoffs. Those three Portland players are responsible for 36 of Portland's goals while Brossoit has only allowed 21. Something has to give there.

Mark Huxley - CanesCast (11-3)

Oil Kings in 7

This, again, is another match up almost too close to call.

I think the difference between the 2 teams will be the belief and confidence that oozes across the oilks roster, that will give them the edge.

Not that we will ever hear the end of this if the oil kings do win, with our good friend Mr Ohashi working for the evil empire ;-)

Morgan Martin - CanesCast (9-5)
