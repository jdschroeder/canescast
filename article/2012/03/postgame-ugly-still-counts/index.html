Postgame: Ugly Still Counts

March 06, 2012 - by Morgan Martin

Tonight’s game sees a rematch with the Warriors who caused the Canes a great deal of trouble on Saturday Night. Tonight is redemption night for the Canes after a couple poor showings on the weekend which saw some unlucky breaks early in the game break their backs. Also of note for Hurricane fans: this will be the final WHL appearance for former Hurricane Cam Braes at the Enmax Centre. Thanks to the continued injury troubles up front, the Hurricanes have called up Reid Duke for his eighth game as an affiliate this season.

What happened

The first period showed a resumption of hostilities early as Spencer Galbraith and Jamie Henry dropped the gloves and had what would be one of the better games this season. Back in form Ketlo made some stellar saves to keep them in the game in the first, something that has been lacking of late. With no bad breaks early in the game the Canes were able to make some plays with some good tips and strong drives to the net. Score was 0 - 0 after one.

Second period play was carried largely by Moose Jaw, at times taking advantage from some sloppy own-zone play by the Hurricanes. Solid play from Ketlo and a number of successful defensive scrambles managed to keep the visitors off the board, though. The Hurricanes made a late push and managed to crack the ice in the final minute of the period when Phil Tot slid a pass toward the net for Russ Maxwell who made a lateral move before sliding a backhand through Luke Siemens’ pads. The Hurricanes took a 1-0 lead to the second intermission.

More penalty trouble early and it seemed like we might be in for a repeat. Ketlo with some great saves and some strong defence in front kept the warriors off the score sheet. The only blip was a one timer off the draw from Beach that Ketlo stood no chance on. The Canes pressure didn't stop, and in this game strong play can be rewarded with good luck. A double tip on a centering attempt from Tot put the puck behind Siemens and the Canes up by 1 (2 - 1). Gutsy play at the end of the game kept the warriors from getting that equilizer.

One good reason

Without a little luck tonight things might not have been the same. A drive to the net and great play by Damien Ketlo gave them the chance for this game.

SportPhotographers.ca Player of the Game

Tonights Player of the Game was Damien Ketlo. Outstanding between the pipes. His play was the big reason that the Canes won.

Wrap-up

The regulation win for the Hurricanes spoils the Warriors’ bid to clinch the eastern division title. Next up the Hurricanes host the Calgary Hitmen on Friday night at 7:00. Bring your chequebook and place a bid on a game worn third jersey.
