First round playoff predictions

March 22, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

The WHL playoffs are upon us, and just because the Hurricanes aren’t there doesn’t mean we can’t have some fun with it. We’ve assembled a panel to join us in picking the winners of the 2012 WHL playoffs.

“Predictions may be the dumbest thing ever - I mean, if the media was any good at forecasting outcomes, we'd all be living in mansions in Vegas -- but they go with the territory.” (Bob McKenzie, TSN Hockey Insider)

With that in mind, let’s see if any of us are any good at forecasting outcomes.

Here are your prognosticators for this year:

  • The CanesCast team: Josh Schroeder, Mark Huxley, and Morgan Martin
  • Lethbridge Hurricanes Communications and PR Manager Esther Madziya
  • 94.1 CJOC Hurricanes play-by-play voice Pat Siedlecki
  • 94.1 CJOC Hurricanes colour commentator Fred Jack
  • CTV Reporter Darrell Romuld
  • Hurricane season ticket holder Keli Hay

On the Oil Kings and Ice

Josh: I like Edmonton’s depth here. Nathan Lieuwen could make the series interesting for the Ice, but the Oil Kings have some solid goaltending of their own in Laurent Brossoit.

Esther: I was going to have the Oil Kings in 5, but Kootenay knows how to win & has good goaltending. They should win 2.

Fred: The Oil Kings draw the defending champs in the opening round. Definitely not the same fire power this year with Kootenay but they are very well coached and have a game changer and a champion in goal in Nathan Lieuwen. Edmonton is much deeper but does not have championship experience. I don't think the Oil Kings will win this series as easy as many others think they will, but they'll find a way on home ice to get it done.

Darrell: I know Edmonton had the skill and has the statistics in the regular season to suggest they'll have a deep run, but I just don't think they match up with kootenay's strength and grit. If Nathan Lieuwen gets hot, and Max and Sam Reinhart get going this series may be over early.

On the Warriors and Pats

Josh: The Warriors are built for the playoffs with plenty of strong veteran leadership. They could go the distance this year.

Esther: Pats will likely split, beacause they won’t win on the road and the Warriors are too good at home, so they only get 1 game. Go Cam Braes!!

Fred: For me, Moose Jaw is the most playoff ready roster out of all Eastern Conference teams. They have tremendous leadership and many of the pieces to go all the way this year. Great rivalry season, the buildings will be a jumping but Moose Jaw is just too much for Pat Conacher (whom I believe should be the Eastern Conference's Coach of the Year (Editor’s note: Good call, Fred. Conacher was indeed the east’s coach of the year)) and his Regina Pats.

Darrell: Moose Jaw is just too good. Plain and simple.

On the Hitmen and Wheat Kings

Josh: Boes has been solid late in the season, but Brandon’s goaltending has been so inconsistent this season. I think Calgary has the upper hand based on the goaltending match-up.

Esther: There’s no better team with a turnaround than Calgary. Boes has been good in goal for Brandon lately, but still goaltending is suspect for them. Calgary’s solid.

Fred: Upset special! Mark Stone is the best player in this series and overall Calgary can't match Brandon's fire power.

Darrell: Evenly matched to me.

On the Tigers and Blades

Josh: Etem and Shinkaruk bring a lot of firepower, and Bunz backs it all up. The Tigers get out of the first round on individual performances.

Esther: The Tigers have Etem & Shinkaruk, but the Hurricanes basically shut them down and won the series. If we can beat them so can Saskatoon. The Blades have better goaltending too – I’ll take Makarov over Bunz any day and the Blades' defence is too big and too good. Besides, the Blades were my team for 8 years, those are my boys & my coaches – I’ll always cheer for them!!

Fred: The three best players in this series all play for Medicine Hat. Saskatoon won't have an answer for Emerson Etem, Hunter Shinkaruk and Tyler Bunz.

Darrell: Saskatoon was a better team this season than their record showed, they've got a lot of talent and a lot more grit than a Medicine Hat team does. Makarov is a good goalie, better than he's given credit for, plus I would never go against my boy Luke Sutter. Blades in 6.

On the Americans and Silvertips

Josh: No way Everett overcomes the huge talent gap here. Americans will cruise by them.

Fred: Tri City is scary good. They will roll through the first round.

Darrell: Patrick Holland and Brendan Shinnimin. Those two players alone will win this series for Tri-City.

On the Blazers and Royals

Josh: Victoria were deadline day sellers. Not enough there to compete with the Blazers this year.

Fred: Great turn around story this year in Kamloops. The coaching duel of Guy Charon and Dave Hunchak are as good as you'll find in the WHL. Blazers will make easy work of the Royals.

On the Winterhawks and Rockets

Josh: Portland are a very good number three seed. It feels to me like the Americans and Winterhawks are on a collision course, and the Rockets won’t be able to prevent it.

Fred: The Winterhawks are the real deal, just like Tri-City. Kelowna will be no match.

Darrell: You saw how good Sven Baertschi is.

On the Giants and Chiefs

Josh: The only series in the west that’s even close is extremely closely matched. Expect a few games to go to overtime here. I think the Giants win with their better top-end scoring ability.

Fred: No way am I betting against Don Hay in the 1st Round. It would be great to see Austin Fyten make a positive impact in this series. 2 good teams, and I expect a long, hard gought series, however in the end the G-Men will prevail.

Darrell: Austin Fyten is a big wildcard for Vancouver.

Thanks to all of our panelists for sharing their predictions with us. We’ll check in with them again before the second round begins.
