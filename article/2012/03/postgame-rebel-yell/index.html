Postgame: Rebel Yell

March 16, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

Since winning their last three encounters with the Red Deer Rebels, the Lethbridge Hurricanes have gone nearly four months without playing their divisional opponent. The Hurricanes looked to put one more in the win column against the injury depleted Rebels.

On the Hurricane side of the line-up card, Brady Ramsay and Albin Blomqvist remain sidelined with injuries while Reid Duke and Tanner Kovacs sit this one out as well. Liam Liston gets the call in goal for the Hurricanes.

What happened

Some back and forth action to start the game, but most of the early pressure came from the Rebels. The Hurricanes, on their heels, found themselves icing the puck regularly in the opening minutes. The game’s first goal came off of a neutral zone turnover by the Hurricanes that led to a Charles Inglis led rush where he fired one from the right wing inside the far post to give Red Deer a 1-0 lead. Neither team was able to establish much on the powerplay, even after Jaimen Yakubowski went to the box for four minutes after taking exception to a Dexter Bricker hit on Juraj Bezuch and jumping the Rebel behind the play. After 20 minutes, the shots were even at 14 apiece, and the Rebels leading 1-0 on the scoreboard.

The Hurricanes pushed back in the second period, driving the net hard early and often. Brody Sutter led the way with a couple of good rushes. On the Canes’ third powerplay opportunity of the game, Macoy Erkamps delivered on a one-timer from Adam Henry, beating Bolton Pouliot high glove side with a rocket of a shot to tie the game at one apiece.

Cody Thiel kicked off the third period, weaving through the Hurricanes’ zone and sniping a backhander over Liston’s right shoulder to give the Rebels a 2-1 lead just over a minute into the period. The Hurricanes answered right back with Craig Leverton stuffing one inside short side post when it looked like Pouliot had everything covered. Rebels’ captain Adam Kambeitz, the Coaldale native who seems to always perform well against the Hurricanes, restored the lead for the home side by collecting a loose puck in the low slot and maneuvering it around Liston. Despite a late powerplay opportunity, then a pulled goalie and plenty of offensive zone possession, the Hurricanes just couldn’t manage to draw back on even terms and ended up dropping a 3-2 decision despite outshooting the Rebels 36-27.

One good reason

...why the Hurricanes lost. A couple of isolated sloppy defensive plays led to the first two Red Deer goals. All-in-all it was a good effort from the Hurricanes, and a game they could have won if not for those couple of mistakes.

SportPhotographers.ca Player of the Game

Macoy Erkamps takes the honour tonight. He was agressive on the puck along the wall and moved the puck well on the powerplay. Matched up with Adam Henry in powerplay situations, the two were able to feed each other for a number of one-timers, one of which resulted in the Hurricanes’ first goal off of Erkamps’ stick.

Wrap-up

71 games down, one to go. The Hurricanes conclude the 2011/12 season tomorrow night in Medicine Hat when they face the Tigers at 7:30.
