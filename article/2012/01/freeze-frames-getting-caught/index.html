Freeze Frames: Getting Caught Up

January 24, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

To get us all caught up, here’s a quick look at the November and December that were for the Lethbridge Hurricanes with some of my favourite photos from that stretch.

November 2 vs. Everett

The Hurricanes snap their October losing streak with a shootout win over the Silvertips

November 9 vs. Seattle and November 11 C-17 Globemaster Tour

Check these photos out in the previous edition of Freeze Frames.

November 12 vs. Tri-City

Jay Merkley celebrates a goal against the Americans in a 7-3 loss.

November 19 vs. Edmonton

Damien Ketlo looks right at us during a 5-2 win over the Oil Kings in which he made 35 saves.

November 23 vs. Edmonton

The 10-3 wasn’t nearly as impressive as Tyler Kizuik’s full Movember moustache.

December 3 vs. Saskatoon

Albin Blomqvist delivers a solid body check on Josh Nicholls. So far, I think this is my favourite photo from the season.

Tyler Wong plays his first of three games with the Hurricanes. It was a good showing from the UFA Bison call-up.

Juraj Bezuch puts his all into a slap shot.

Damien Ketlo gets his pad on a wrap-around attempt for one of his 49 stops on the way to a 6-3 win.

Jay Merlkey one-times a centering pass in for a third period goal to give the Hurricanes a 5-2 lead.

Brody Sutter faces off against cousin Lukas, who calls Lethbridge home.

The Hurricanes salute the fans after another big win.

December 4 vs. Spokane

Jamal Watson is poke checked on a breakaway by Mac Engel.

Twister celebrates a goal in the mascot game during an intermission.

Captain Cam Braes leads the team in a salute of the fans after a 1-0 win.

Cam Braes and Brady Ramsay high five to celebrate the win.

Liam Liston high fives some fans after earning a 38 save shut out win over the Chiefs.

December 14 vs. Calgary

Damien Ketlo was looking good as he debuted some new paint against the Hitmen.

Brady Brassart goes one on one with Ketlo.

Spencer Galbraith delivers his signature hip check on Jimmy Bubnick.

Graham Hood blasts one from the left side, scoring his first of two goals on the evening.

The Hurricanes celebrate Graham Hood’s overtime winner.

December 16 vs. Victoria

We had some help on this night as a couple of young fans gave us a hand with our shooting.

It was Mark who captured this moment, though; Brody Sutter celebrating a goal in the first period, complete with the glow of the red light in the background.

And one of our aspiring photographers snapped this shot of Sutter protecting the puck in the Royals’ zone.

December 17 vs. Swift Current

Teddy Bear night against the Broncos. No scoring during the opening 20 minutes, but here’s looking down the Hurricanes’ bench during a first period time-out.

Early in the second period the Hurricanes open the scoring and the bears begin their descent.

Graham Hood celebrates with the bench as the bears continue to come down.

Here’s the scorer of the big goal, Graham Hood, along with Phil Tot and Brody Sutter who provided the assists.

Twister help collect the bears.

Damien Ketlo starts back to his crease amid a pile of bears and an RCMP car filled with more bears.

The last of the bears are cleaned off the ice.

Late in the game, Cam Braes and Graham Hood congratulate Albin Blomqvist on scoring his first WHL goal.

That takes us to the Christmas break. There’s lots of hockey yet to come, and I’ll try to keep up with the Freeze Frame posts a little more regularly in 2012.

Josh Schroeder

Josh’s hockey life has been intertwined with the Hurricanes since he took to the ice for training camp in 1999. Since then, he has spent countless hours in the Enmax Centre, occupying seats in section OO, the press box, and that tiny space between the players’ benches. In 2011 he designed the Hurricanes’ popular Hawker Hurricane third jersey emblem.

As a member of the CanesCast team, Josh builds and maintains CanesCast.ca, writes articles, and shoots game photos.
