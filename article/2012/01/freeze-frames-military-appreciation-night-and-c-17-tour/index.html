Freeze Frames: Military Appreciation Night and C-17 Tour

January 19, 2012 - by Josh Schroeder

It’s been a while since I've done a Freeze Frame article, and that means that I’ve neglected some photos that I was pretty excited to share from back in November. On November 9th the Hurricanes hosted the Seattle Thunderbirds on Military Appreciation Night.

Prior to the game was an on-ice ceremony paying tribute to the Canadian Forces:

The Hurricanes suited up in special edition uniforms featuring a camouflage pattern, yellow ribbons, and maple leafs replacing the stars on the logo.

Cam Braes:

Spencer Galbraith:

Tyler Kizuik:

Rich Preston’s Movember duster was already in full force:

Oh, and there was a hockey game played, too. Here’s Braes looking for a shooting lane through the T-Birds’ defence:

Graham Hood delivers a hit at the blue line:

Damien Ketlo kicks one just wide of the net (incidently, I think Ketlo’s old Pats mask went very well with those uniforms):

Sam Mckechnie tips a puck in, just under the crossbar:

And the Hurricanes celebrate a goal:

Unfortunately the Hurricanes fell to the Thunderbirds by a 3-2 score on that night. The full set of 47 photos from that game can be seen in the game set on our Flickr account.

A couple of days later the Hurricanes paid a Remembrance Day visit to the airport for a tour of a C-17 Globemaster aircraft. I tagged along to get some photos of that event as well, and enjoyed seeing just how enormous those planes are.

When we first climbed aboard the Globemaster we received a quick introduction to the aircraft and how the Canadian Forces use it. (You do know that the kind of American-looking flag hanging in there is the flag of Lethbridge, right?)

The players then set out to explore:

It seems the highlight for most of the guys was getting to sit in the cockpit, usually posing for a photo or two while there. Here we have Mckechnie and Watson at the controls:

And before it was all said and done, we posed the guys for a few photos. In this one, the team stands in the cargo area while I look down from the bubble behind the cockpit:

This really doesn’t do justice to how wide the cargo area is. They can drive a pair of 18-wheelers in there side-by-side.

For a few more photos from the C-17 tour you can check out the Flickr set.

We’ve got plenty more photos that haven’t been showcased here yet, so watch for another edition of Freeze Frames early next week before the guys return to action on Wednesday.

Josh’s hockey life has been intertwined with the Hurricanes since he took to the ice for training camp in 1999.
